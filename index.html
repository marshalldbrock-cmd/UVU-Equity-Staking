Our Mission

H4U (HomeOwnership4U) exists to close the homeownership gap for Utah County working families. We partner directly with cities to apply a proven, data-driven playbook that removes unnecessary cost from the development process — so a family earning $100K–$150K can actually afford to buy a home in the community where they work.

H4U Fee Disclosure
H4U charges a 4% development fee on all hard costs (land, horizontal, vertical, impact fees) to cover organizational operations. This fee is disclosed in the H4U Net Price and is not hidden in the lever savings.

H4U also charges a 3% commission based on appraised value (CMA), not the sale price, at closing. Both fees are shown explicitly in the Results tab waterfall.
Key Terms
PITI
Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance — the total monthly housing payment lenders use to qualify borrowers.
DTI
Debt-to-Income Ratio — the percentage of gross monthly income toward housing. FHA front-end guideline is 31%.
Silent Second Lien
A subordinated loan (often city/RDA funded) that reduces the buyer's first mortgage without increasing monthly payments to a second servicer. Improves affordability without appearing in the first PITI.
CMA
Comparative Market Analysis — an estimate of a property's market value based on comparable recent sales. H4U's 3% commission is based on this figure.
RDA
Redevelopment Agency — a city government tool using tax increment financing and surplus property to subsidize land costs for affordable or workforce housing.
Hard Costs
Land, horizontal development, vertical construction, and impact/permit fees — the basis for H4U's 4% development fee.
